Rooftop Bars & Nightlife
LIV at Fontainebleau, Broken Shaker, WATR Rooftop at Kimpton — Miami nights are legendary and this is your only chance

Curated Experiences
01
The 22 Historic Squares
Wander one of America's most beautiful cities on foot — verdant, moss-draped squares each with their own character and history
02
Forsyth Park
The iconic fountain, Spanish moss hanging from ancient oaks, Saturday farmers market. The perfect morning walk
03
Ghost Tour of Old Savannah
One of America's most haunted cities. Evening ghost walking tours are atmospheric, entertaining and genuinely unsettling
04
River Street & Riverboat Cruise
Cobblestone waterfront packed with bars, restaurants and shops. A sunset riverboat cruise is a superb group evening
05
Tybee Island Beach
30-minute drive to a stunning barrier island — the famous red-and-white lighthouse, clean beaches, excellent seafood shacks
06
Dolphin & Wildlife Cruise
Bull River Cruises — guided eco-cruise through the coastal marshes spotting dolphins, turtles and shorebirds
07
Bonaventure Cemetery
Hauntingly beautiful Victorian cemetery overlooking the Wilmington River — featured in Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil
08
Southern Lowcountry Dining
The Olde Pink House (1771), Mrs. Wilkes Dining Room, Local 11ten — some of the finest Southern cooking in Georgia

Stop Four  ·  26–28 September  ·  ✦ Feature Destination
🏖️ Hilton Head Island
🌙 2 Nights
🏠 Airbnb
⭐ Most Time Here
September on Hilton Head — The crowds of summer have departed but the ocean remains warm (avg 82°F / 28°C). This is arguably the finest time of year on the island. With 40 miles of bike paths, world-class golf courses including the legendary Harbour Town Links, boat rentals, dolphin cruises, kayaking and some of the most beautiful beaches on the East Coast — two nights barely scratches the surface. Consider adding a third.
Curated Experiences
01
Pontoon Boat Rental
H2O Sports or Outside Hilton Head — hire a pontoon for the day and explore the waterways, inlets and sandbars at your own pace
02
Golf — Harbour Town Links
One of the most celebrated courses in American golf, designed by Pete Dye. Sea Pines Resort offers multiple world-class layouts
03
Private Dolphin Cruise
Guided eco-cruise through the creeks and sounds — dolphin encounters are virtually guaranteed. Highly rated by all visitors
04
Coligny Beach Park
The island's most popular beach — wide, clean, with excellent nearby dining and beach shops. Perfect for a full beach day
05
Cycling the Island
40+ miles of dedicated bike paths through maritime forest and along the beach — rent bikes and explore at leisure
06
Sunset Kayak Tour
Paddle through Broad Creek and the sea marshes at golden hour — a genuinely magical experience in the Lowcountry landscape
07
Harbour Town Lighthouse
The iconic red-and-white striped lighthouse of Sea Pines — climb for panoramic views, browse the boutiques below
08
Skull Creek Boathouse Dining
The finest waterfront seafood restaurant on the island — fresh Lowcountry shrimp, crab, oysters at sunset on the water

Stop Five  ·  28–29 September
🏔️ Smoky Mountains
🌙 1 Night
🏠 Mountain Cabin / Airbnb
📍 Gatlinburg, TN
Curated Experiences
01
Great Smoky Mountains National Park
America's most visited national park — entirely free entry. 800+ miles of trails, waterfalls, wildlife drives and views of rolling blue ridges
02
Gatlinburg SkyBridge
North America's longest pedestrian suspension bridge — 680 feet above the valley floor. Breathtaking and genuinely thrilling
03
Ole Smoky Moonshine Distillery
America's most visited distillery — sample the legendary moonshine flights in the heart of downtown Gatlinburg
04
Dollywood — Pigeon Forge
15 minutes from Gatlinburg — a genuinely world-class theme park featuring Lightning Rod, the world's fastest wooden roller coaster
05
White Water Rafting — Pigeon River
Class III-IV rapids with multiple operators — exhilarating, accessible and the ideal group activity
06
Cades Cove Wildlife Drive
11-mile loop through an open valley — black bears, white-tailed deer and wild turkeys seen regularly in their natural habitat
07
Alum Cave Trail
The most popular hike in the Smokies — 4.4 miles round trip through ancient forest to dramatic overhanging bluffs
08
Anakeesta Mountaintop Park
Aerial tram to a mountaintop village with a tree canopy walk, zip-lines, fire pits and a restaurant with panoramic ridge views

Stop Six  ·  29 Sep – 1 Oct  ·  ✈ Fly Home
🎸 Nashville
🌙 2 Nights
🏠 Airbnb
✈️ Fly Home 1 October
Curated Experiences
01
Broadway Honky-Tonks
Tootsies Orchid Lounge, Robert's Western World, Legends Corner — live music from noon to 3am, never a cover charge. Non-negotiable
02
Royal Range USA — Firing Range
Southeast's largest indoor range (40,000 sq ft, 31 lanes). Machine guns, pistols, rifles — group bookings and bachelor party packages available
03
Country Music Hall of Fame + RCA Studio B
Where Elvis, Dolly Parton and hundreds of legends recorded their most iconic tracks. The studio tour is genuinely excellent
04
Jack Daniel's Distillery
One hour south in Lynchburg, TN — the world's most famous whiskey, produced in the only dry county in Tennessee. A superb half-day
05
Grand Ole Opry
The historic home of country music — Friday and Saturday night live shows are the gold standard. Book tickets well in advance
06
Nashville Hot Chicken
Prince's Hot Chicken (the 1945 original) or Hattie B's — the spicy iconic dish invented right here. An essential Nashville rite of passage
07
White Limozeen Rooftop Bar
The retro-pink rooftop atop Graduate Nashville — one of the most-photographed venues in the city and a perfect closing evening
08
Printer's Alley
Nashville's historic entertainment alley — intimate cocktail bars, jazz, and a genuine local atmosphere beyond the Broadway tourist strip
