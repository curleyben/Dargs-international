Stop One · 22–23 September
MIAMI
1 night · Hotel · 6 Dargs let loose in South Beach. What could go wrong.
Things to Do That Aren't Just the Hotel Bar
01
South Beach & Ocean Drive
The iconic strip — Art Deco hotels, outdoor cafés, the beach boardwalk. Good luck getting six Dargs moving before noon though.
02
Wynwood Walls
World-famous outdoor street art district. Free entry, extraordinary murals, and a great place for Slim to pretend he knows about art.
03
Art Deco Historic District
800+ preserved 1920s-40s buildings — the largest Art Deco concentration on earth. Chetwood will definitely over-explain this to everyone.
04
Little Havana — Calle Ocho
Cuban coffee, cigars, street dominos. Maccers will somehow spend £80 on a cigar he doesn't smoke.
05
Sunset Biscayne Bay Boat Tour
Celebrity mansions, dolphins, sunset over the water. Ideal first-night activity before Gothmog drags everyone to a club at 2am.
06
Rooftop Bars & Nightlife
LIV, Broken Shaker, WATR Rooftop. Miami nights are legendary. David will be asleep by 11pm. The rest of you: good luck.

Stop Two · 23–24 September
JACKSONVILLE
1 night · Airbnb or Hotel · 6 guests · Halfway to Savannah, basically.
Things to Actually Do Here
01
Jacksonville Beach
Wide, clean Atlantic beach. Perfect post-5-hour-drive activity. Gothmog will immediately attempt to bodyboard.
02
Riverside Arts District
Galleries, restaurants, live music along the St. Johns River. David's type of evening. Everyone else will follow reluctantly.
03
St. Johns River Sunset Cruise
Florida's longest river, dolphins, golden hour. Someone will fall asleep on the boat. We all know who.
04
San Marco District Bars & Dining
Elegant neighbourhood with excellent restaurants and cocktail bars. Civilised. Slim will ruin it.
05
Cummer Museum of Art & Gardens
Stunning waterfront museum and Italianate gardens. Chetwood will insist. The others will pretend they're enjoying it.
06
Hemming Park Food Trucks
Downtown food trucks, craft beer, good vibes. Low effort, high reward. The Dargs natural habitat.

Stop Three · 24–26 September
SAVANNAH
2 nights · Airbnb · 6 guests · America's most haunted city. Right up Gothmog's street.
The Highlights
01
The 22 Historic Squares
One of America's most beautiful cities on foot. Spanish moss, ancient oaks, gorgeous architecture. Even Maccers will admit it's lovely.
02
Forsyth Park
Iconic fountain, Saturday farmers market. David's kind of morning. Everyone else arrives two hours later with coffees.
03
Ghost Tour (Evening)
Savannah is one of America's most haunted cities. Gothmog has already looked this up. You're all doing this whether you like it or not.
04
River Street & Riverboat Cruise
Cobblestone waterfront, bars, sunset cruise. Classic. Chetwood will bring a book and read it on the boat deck.
05
Tybee Island Beach
30-min drive — brilliant beach, lighthouse you can climb, great seafood shacks. Slim's second attempt at surfing.
06
Dolphin & Wildlife Cruise
Guided eco-cruise through the coastal marshes. Dolphins virtually guaranteed. Curley has already done research on this one.
07
Bonaventure Cemetery
Hauntingly beautiful Victorian cemetery — featured in Midnight in the Garden of Good and Evil. Genuinely stunning in an unsettling way.
08
Southern Dining
The Olde Pink House (1771), Mrs. Wilkes Dining Room — legendary Southern cooking. This is not the night to be fussy, David.

Stop Four · 26–28 September · ★ THE BIG ONE
HILTON HEAD
2 nights (should be 3) · Airbnb · 6 guests · Beaches, boats, golf, dolphins. The Dargs in their element.
Heads up — September on Hilton Head is as good as it gets. Ocean's still warm (82°F / 28°C), summer crowds gone, dolphins everywhere. With a pontoon boat rental you can basically do a full day's exploring yourselves — creeks, sandbars, the lot. Strongly recommend adding a third night here — the beaches alone justify it and it's only 45 minutes from Savannah.
The Activities — Don't Waste This Stop
01
Rent a Pontoon Boat
H2O Sports or Outside Hilton Head — hire a pontoon for the day, captain it yourselves around the waterways and sandbars. Absolute highlight of the trip. Maccers will somehow capsize it.
02
Golf — Harbour Town Links
One of the most famous golf courses in America — home of the RBC Heritage PGA Tour event. Sea Pines Resort has multiple world-class layouts. Chetwood has been talking about this since February.
03
Dolphin Watching Cruise
Guided eco-cruise — dolphins are practically guaranteed. Even Slim, who claims to hate dolphins, will love this.
04
Coligny Beach
The island's main beach — wide, gorgeous, clean. Spend a full afternoon here. Gothmog will burn immediately.
05
Cycling the Island
40+ miles of bike paths through maritime forest and along the shore. Rent bikes for a morning. David will be suspiciously good at this.
06
Sunset Kayak Tour
Through Broad Creek and the sea marshes at golden hour. If you do one organised activity on this trip, make it this.
07
Harbour Town Lighthouse
The iconic red and white striped lighthouse at Sea Pines — climb it for the views, browse the marina. Classic photo spot.
08
Skull Creek Boathouse Dining
Best waterfront restaurant on the island — fresh shrimp, crab, oysters on the water at sunset. Book ahead. Worth it.

Stop Five · 28–29 September
SMOKY MOUNTAINS
1 night · Mountain Cabin / Airbnb · Gatlinburg, TN · America's most visited national park. Absolutely free.
What To Do In the Mountains
01
Great Smoky Mountains National Park
Most visited national park in the US. FREE. 800+ miles of trails, waterfalls, wildlife drives, endless views. Gothmog will attempt a summit in flip-flops.
02
Gatlinburg SkyBridge
North America's longest pedestrian suspension bridge — 680 feet above the valley. Breathtaking. Slim will absolutely bottle halfway across.
03
Ole Smoky Moonshine Distillery
America's most visited distillery, in the middle of downtown Gatlinburg. Moonshine tasting flights. This is happening regardless of what David thinks.
04
Dollywood — Pigeon Forge
World-class theme park 15 minutes away. Lightning Rod is the world's fastest wooden roller coaster. Nobody will admit they're scared. They're all scared.
05
White Water Rafting — Pigeon River
Class III–IV rapids with multiple local operators. Excellent group activity. Chetwood will read the safety briefing very carefully.
06
Cades Cove Wildlife Drive
11-mile loop — black bears, deer, wild turkeys in the open valley. Maccers will try to feed a bear. Someone stop him.
07
Alum Cave Trail
Most popular hike in the Smokies — 4.4 miles, spectacular bluffs, ancient forest. David will finish first and look completely fine. Annoying.
08
Anakeesta Mountaintop Park
Aerial tram to a mountaintop village — tree canopy walk, zip-lines, fire pits, mountain-view restaurant. Great last night before Nashville.

Stop Six · 29 Sep – 1 Oct · ✈ FLY HOME
NASHVILLE
2 nights · Airbnb · Firing ranges. Honky-tonks. Jack Daniel's. Hot chicken. The Dargs farewell tour.
The Grand Finale Activities
01
Broadway Honky-Tonks
Tootsies, Robert's Western World, Legends Corner — live music from noon to 3am, never a cover charge. Non-negotiable. All six Dargs, immediately upon arrival.
02
Royal Range USA — Firing Range
Southeast's largest indoor range — 40,000 sq ft, 31 lanes, machine guns, rifles, pistols. Group packages available. Gothmog has been looking forward to this since day one of planning.
03
Country Music Hall of Fame + RCA Studio B
Where Elvis and Dolly recorded. The studio tour is genuinely excellent. David will actually read every single information panel. Everyone else: 45 minutes.
04
Jack Daniel's Distillery Tour
One hour south in Lynchburg, TN — the world's most famous whiskey, made in the only dry county in Tennessee. The irony. The whiskey. Both excellent.
05
Grand Ole Opry
The historic home of country music, live on Friday/Saturday nights. Book ahead. Slim will pretend he came here specifically for this. He did not.
06
Nashville Hot Chicken
Prince's Hot Chicken (the 1945 original) or Hattie B's. Order it hotter than you think you can handle. This is a Dargs mandatory challenge.
07
White Limozeen Rooftop Bar
Retro-pink rooftop atop Graduate Nashville. The most-photographed spot in the city. Maccers will make everyone take the same photo twelve times.
08
Printer's Alley
Nashville's historic cocktail alley — intimate bars, jazz, actual locals. The final night deserves something beyond the tourist strip. Finish here.
